About us

Introduction to Shri Amreli Muk Badhir Seva Trust Managed by Shrimati Tapiben Mohanlal Rugnath Mehta School for the Deaf and Dumb

Introduction

Introduction to Shri Amreli Muk Badhir Seva Trust.

With a rich history spanning over twenty-three years, our organization stands as a testament to the invaluable support from the people of the Amreli region. The inspiration for our institution was ignited by Shri Mangaldasbhai Mehta, who, upon reading an article by Shri Rajni Kumar Pandya in a Sunday edition of Janmabhoomi, envisioned a similar service initiative in his hometown. This idea took root after his visit to a deaf and dumb school in Jetpur during his travels from Mumbai via Rajkot to Amreli. Upon his return to Amreli, he shared his experiences with us, leading to a collective visit to the Jetpur institution. Subsequently, the Amreli Muk Badhir Seva Trust was established with Shri Mangaldas Mehta as the founding president, along with notable trustees like Shri Natvarbhai Vora, Dr. Pratabhai Panchal, Shri Mukundbhai Mehta, Shri Becharbhai Pokal, Shri Dineshbhai Gandhi, and Shri Kishorbhai Mehta as managing trustees

Our Beginnings

From Humble Beginnings to a Beacon of Hope.

Guided by Dr. Manaseta, a retired teacher, and local volunteer Pragati Ben Vaidh, the school began its journey. Within a few years, it became evident that Amreli district had over 800 deaf and mute children, primarily from laborer families or those engaged in small businesses. This realization led to the decision to establish a residential school with a hostel to provide comprehensive care and education, entirely free of charge. During this period, it was discovered that the Vora Kamani Hostel under the Amreli District Vidyasabha had been closed for a long time. An appeal to the Vidyasabha resulted in the generous provision of a 42-room building, dining hall, and adjoining land for the Trust's use.

Growth and Development

A Journey of Growth and Generosity.

Over the years, our institution has faced numerous challenges, including the need for significant repairs to the hostel building. Shri Mangaldasbhai Mehta publicly donated INR 1.5 million, leading to the renaming of the school as 'Shrimati Tapiben Mohanlal Rugnath Mehta School for the Deaf and Dumb.' Additional funds were raised through generous contributions from donors in Mumbai, including approximately INR 700,000 from various philanthropists. The building was renovated, and on October 20, 2005, the institution was inaugurated by the esteemed donor Shri Dipchandbhai Gardiya. Since then, the school has continued its development journey.

Support and Expansion.

Further support came from Shri Kamleshbhai Parekh, who donated INR 1.1 million, and the vocational training program began in the name of 'Shrimati Rasilaaben Harkishanadas Parekh.' The institution also started receiving government grants for staff salaries and meal expenses for up to 50 children, despite our average annual student count being 75. In 2015, after the passing of Shri Mangaldasbhai and his wife Hansaben, their sons, Shri Prashantbhai and Shri Malaybhai, continued their active involvement and donations, leading to the construction of 'Shrimati Hansaben Mangaldas Mehta Bhavan,' a separate hostel for students. The establishment of the Audiology and Speech Therapy Clinic by Shrimati Meenakshiben Vinaybhai Valiya, along with ongoing donations from various philanthropists like Shri Vikrambhai Gordia, Shri Induben Sanghvi, and many others, has been instrumental in our growth.

Community and Support

Community-Driven and Supported.

Significant contributions have come from the Rotary Club of Amreli Gir and Rotary International, who provided INR 2.8 million for various equipment for the Audiology and Speech Therapy Clinic. PGVCL has donated INR 1 million for a smart board. Shri Chimanbhai Mehta (Deepak Group) has contributed approximately INR 4 million. Numerous small business owners, vendors, and farmers from Amreli have continuously supported our cause with their generous donations, making our organization a unique entity whose responsibility has been seamlessly embraced by the people of Amreli.
